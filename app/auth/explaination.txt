The Big Picture: The Difference Roles
The confusion comes from mixing up The Front Gate (how a user logs in) with The Internal Checkpoints (how a logged-in user moves around your app).

Google OAuth is only used at the Front Gate. It runs exactly once when the user clicks "Login with Google". Once your backend verifies their Google token and finds them in your database, Google's job is done.

Your Custom JWT System is used at the Internal Checkpoints. Your backend wraps the user's ID into your own app's custom JWT passport. For the rest of the session, whenever the user clicks buttons to borrow books or view profiles, your backend ignores Google completely and just verifies your own custom passport.

utils.py holds the math formulas, router.py handles the login gates, and dependencies.py holds the guards protecting your secure data endpoints. They are the trifecta of a secure FastAPI backend.
